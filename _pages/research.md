My research interests lie at the intersection of **energy systems**, **well integrity**, **carbon management**, and **techno-economic analysis**. I am especially interested in work that connects engineering, economics, and real-world infrastructure challenges.

## Current Research Areas

### Well Cement Sealing and Well Integrity
I am working on research related to cement formulation and sealing performance for abandoned and orphan wells in Oklahoma. This work focuses on improving long-term well integrity and exploring practical, cost-effective approaches to well plugging. I am particularly interested in how engineering design choices can improve durability, reduce leakage risk, and support responsible energy infrastructure management.

### Computational Catalysis and Energy Research
I am interested in computational and energy-focused research that connects technical modeling with real-world applications in sustainable energy and carbon management. This includes research areas where chemistry, engineering, and computation can be used together to better understand material performance and energy-related processes.

### Energy Systems and Techno-Economic Analysis
I am also interested in research that combines engineering with economics, especially in cases where technical performance, cost, and infrastructure decisions are tightly connected. I am drawn to questions that require both quantitative reasoning and practical decision-making.

## Research Themes

Across my work, I am most interested in a few broader questions:

- How can engineering research improve the reliability and long-term performance of energy infrastructure?
- How can technical innovation be evaluated not only by performance, but also by cost, scalability, and real-world feasibility?
- How can energy systems be designed and managed more responsibly in a changing economic and environmental landscape?

## Ongoing Direction

My long-term goal is to build expertise in areas where **engineering**, **energy systems**, and **economic thinking** intersect. Through research, I hope to contribute to practical solutions in carbon management, infrastructure resilience, and the future of energy.
